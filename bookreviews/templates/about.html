About The Tortoise Book Prize

The Tortoise Book Prize was founded in 1972. It is awarded annually to the author(s) of an oustanding work of children's fiction published in the United Kingdom. Past winners include JK Rowling, Michael Morpurgo, Jacqueline Wilson and many more.

The nominees are announced in the early Spring of each year, with the prize awarded early June. This gives ample opportunity for each nominated book to be read.

To encourage participation in the Award, the Review scheme takes place annually, where teachers may register their classes as reviewers. Students may read the nominated books and share their thoughts by publishing reviews on this site. The Review project has taken place since 2005 and has successfully created a thoughtful space for students to learn to critically analyse books via their reviews.
